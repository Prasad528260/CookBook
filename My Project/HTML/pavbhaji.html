Pav Bhaji

Pav Bhaji is a popular Indian street food consisting of a spicy vegetable mash (bhaji) served with buttered, toasted buns (pav). It originated in Mumbai and has since become a favorite across India. Here’s a detailed recipe:

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

6 servings

instructions

1. Prepare the Vegetables:

Boil the potatoes, cauliflower, peas, and carrots together in a pot until soft. Mash them and set aside.

2. Cook the Bhaji:

  • Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  • Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  • Add the ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chilies. Cook for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  • Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and the oil starts to separate from the mixture.
  • Add the finely chopped green bell pepper and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  • Now, add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and pav bhaji masala. Stir well to mix the spices.
  • Add the mashed vegetables (potatoes, cauliflower, peas, and carrots) to the pan. Mix everything thoroughly.
  • Add water as needed to adjust the consistency of the bhaji. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  • Season with salt and add 1 tablespoon of butter. Let it simmer for a few more minutes until everything is well combined.

    • 3. Toast the Pav:

  • Heat a flat pan or tawa on medium heat.
  • Slice the pav buns in half horizontally.
  • Spread butter on the cut sides of the pav and toast them on the pan until golden and crisp.

    • 4. Serve:

  • Serve the hot bhaji topped with a dollop of butter, garnished with chopped coriander leaves.
  • Serve with the toasted pav buns, lemon wedges, and finely chopped onions on the side.

    • ingredients

    2 medium potatoes (boiled and mashed)

    1 cup cauliflower florets (boiled and mashed)

    1/2 cup green peas (boiled)

    1/2 cup carrots (boiled and mashed)

    1 green bell pepper (finely chopped)

    1 large onion (finely chopped)

    2 tomatoes (finely chopped)

    2-3 green chilies (chopped)

    1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste

    1 tablespoon pav bhaji masala (available in stores)

    1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder

    1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust as per taste)

    Salt to taste

    1-2 tablespoons butter

    2 tablespoons oil

    Fresh coriander leaves (chopped for garnish)
